# UI Research Notes

FAYE should use these references as product direction, not as layouts to copy.

## Peru Problem Frame

MINAM frames the core gap clearly: Peru recycles only 2.1% of generated waste even though the recoverable potential reaches 78%. The stated causes are limited segregation culture and insufficient differentiated collection infrastructure. Source: https://www.gob.pe/institucion/minam/campa%C3%B1as/106333-recicla-ya-educar-para-reciclar

For FAYE, that means the product should not stop at recognition. The demo should show the full behavior loop: remove doubt, explain the action, log the habit, and give immediate feedback.

## Reference Signals

- Bower: the strongest loop is scan, sort, reward, and visible CO2 impact. Its product promise is immediate action plus progress, which maps well to FAYE's demo flow. Source: https://getbower.com/
- Recycle Coach: the strongest idea is local certainty. It combines fast sorting guidance, collection schedules, reminders, and municipal context. Source: https://www.recyclecoach.com/
- Litterati: the strongest idea is turning each captured item into data that can inform policy, infrastructure, and packaging decisions. Source: https://www.litterati.org/
- Olio: the strongest idea is community value. Waste reduction feels social because nearby people can give, receive, and reuse items. Source: https://olioapp.com/en/

## Direction For FAYE

FAYE should feel like a compact environmental operating system:

- One screen, one loop: input, AI decision, action, habit proof.
- Eve stays invisible. The interface should show intelligence through confidence, uncertainty, and next action instead of a chat bubble.
- Desktop can work like a focused editor: left rail for mode/state, center canvas for image input, right inspector for decision and habit.
- Mobile should be a stepper: camera/input first, then result, then log habit. Avoid showing every panel at once.
- The first screen should make the app understandable in 5 seconds: "scan waste, get the right action, build the habit."

## Demo Priorities

1. The app must reject empty or irrelevant input instead of classifying anything.
2. The image result should feel like an AI observation: what FAYE saw, what it is probably made of, what to do now.
3. Habit feedback must be visible immediately after action: points, streak, logged impact.
4. Local rules should be phrased carefully until district data exists: "check your local point" instead of fake municipal certainty.
